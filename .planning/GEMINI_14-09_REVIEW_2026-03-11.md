## External Suggestions and Adjudication

1. **Taint tracking for derived outputs**
   - Decision: `APPROVE`
   - Reason: Necessary to stop low-trust artifacts from bypassing policy by being rephrased by the model.

2. **Cryptographic signatures on trusted memory**
   - Decision: `APPROVE WITH MODIFICATION`
   - Reason: Full row signing is heavier than needed for first rollout. Tamper-evident signing of promotion/trust-transition events is the right first implementation.

3. **Approval scopes to reduce fatigue**
   - Decision: `APPROVE WITH GUARDRAILS`
   - Reason: Useful, but only as bounded scopes. No broad persistent auto-whitelisting by default.

4. **Quarantine TTL**
   - Decision: `APPROVE`
   - Reason: Prevents storage bloat while preserving semantic learning.

5. **Sandboxed/hardened summarization path**
   - Decision: `APPROVE`
   - Reason: Summaries are an attack surface if generated directly from hostile artifacts with no taint controls.

6. **Offline queue limits and backpressure**
   - Decision: `APPROVE`
   - Reason: Required for mobile robustness and to avoid queue/storage exhaustion.

7. **Mission pause/resume during approval waits**
   - Decision: `APPROVE`
   - Reason: Prevents approval gates from causing mission timeout regressions.

8. **Explicit sync conflict protocol**
   - Decision: `APPROVE`
   - Reason: Trust downgrades and blocked-state propagation must beat stale promotion on mobile.

9. **Approval friction telemetry**
   - Decision: `APPROVE`
   - Reason: Without this, security gates can silently become a UX tax.

10. **Emergency reversion / glass-break downgrade**
    - Decision: `APPROVE`
    - Reason: Needed for post-promotion threat discovery and downstream invalidation.

## Net Result

Gemini's review improved the plan in the right direction. No recommendation required a rewrite of the core architecture. The strongest additions were:

- taint propagation for derived outputs
- tamper-evident trust transitions
- bounded approval scopes
- queue/backpressure/conflict handling on Android
- glass-break revocation

The plan should continue using the current architectural stance:

- learning always-on
- promotion conditional
- shadow mode before enforcement
- retrieval ranking before suppression
